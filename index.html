About Me

My name is Richard, and I’m a Registered Massage Therapist.

Before moving into the manual therapy space, I was a strength coach for 15 years, working with athletes of all levels and across a broad variety of sports. I earned my Bachelors of Science in Kinesiology from Dalhousie University; my degree mainly focused on biomechanics and exercise physiology, with neuroscience rounding out my education. I have a deep interest in human movement, sport, performance, health and wellbeing. I fundamentally believe that movement matters in our lives, whether that movement is to express ourselves, challenge our physical limits, or invigorate the mind. Once upon a time I was a competitive athlete as well - though these days I maintain my fitness to enjoy life and to be the best therapist I can be.

What I Do

My therapy is clinically focused. I enjoy working with anyone, though it should be known that relaxation is not typically the focus of my sessions (though some may find my therapy style relaxing). Through my sessions we’ll be looking to gain mobility, manage acute injury, remedy chronic injury, deal with scar tissue, and to adopt changes from the therapy into how you move or your posture. I’m confident in using myofascial and deep tissue techniques, and tackling TMJ, neck, and headache issues, along with maintenance work for athletes.

In sessions, I typically do not use sheets. For TMJ and neck/headache clients, you can wear whatever is comfortable, but a tank-top or something similar would be the best. For other areas of therapeutic focus, athletic wear such as shorts and a t-shirt (a sports bra for female clients if available) work best. I prefer this because during the session we’ll be incorporating stretching, and you may be changing positions often. Shorts and a shirt makes this easy and ensures that you get the most therapy in the time we have. If you prefer to use sheets, just let me know before our session and we can use sheets.
